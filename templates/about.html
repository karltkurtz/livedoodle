About

// What it is
LiveDoodle is a real-time drawing experiment. You open it on your phone, pick up a brush, and start drawing. Your strokes appear live on a 7" Raspberry Pi screen sitting in my house. You can even add your own images to the drawings! — no account, no install, no delay.
// How it works
Your phone sends each stroke over a WebSocket as it happens. The server relays it instantly to the Raspberry Pi display. A second Pi running a camera films that screen and streams the feed back to you — so the snapshot on the home page is a live photo of real hardware, not a simulation.
// The setup
YOUR PHONE draws via pigarage.com
↓ websocket
PI A Raspberry Pi 4 — runs the server, drives the 7" display
↓ HDMI
7" SCREEN your art, rendered full screen in my house
↓ camera
PI B Raspberry Pi 4 — HQ camera pointed at the display, streams JPEG snapshots
↓ tunnel
YOU see the live feed on the home page
